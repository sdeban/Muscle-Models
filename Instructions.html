Muscle Explorer

Overview

The model simulates the interaction of several elements: a muscle-like actuator, an inertial muscle mass, a damper, a hookean spring, a lever with changing mechanical advantange, and an inertial projectile mass. The muscle begins activation at time zero, builds force and shortens, stretching the spring. The spring is attached by a massless lever to a projectile. You can specify many properties such as the Vmax and P0 of the muscle, the mass of the projectile, the length of the lever (i.e., the mechanical advantage or gear ratio), and the conditions under which the lever is allowed to move.
