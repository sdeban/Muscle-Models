Muscle Explorer

Overview

Muscle Explorer simulates the interaction of several elements: a muscle-like actuator, an inertial muscle mass, a damper, a hookean spring, a massless lever with adjustable mechanical advantange, and an inertial projectile mass. Physiological and biomechanical parameters (listed below) can be set and when the simulation is run, activation of the muscle actuator begins. The muscle actuator builds force and moves the inertial muscle mass, stretching the spring, which is attached by the massless lever to a projectile mass.

Purpose

The purpose of this model is to simulate the performance of musculoskeletal systems in which a single muscle shortens against an in series elastic element and an inertial "projectile" mass. The model can be used to simulate muscle-tendon unit behavior, after-loaded in-vitro muscle protocols, or the performance of a virtual jumping organism.

Simulation method

Forward dynamics using a classical fourth-order Runge-Kutta method.
